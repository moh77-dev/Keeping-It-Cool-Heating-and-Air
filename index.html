The honest question

Should you repair it or replace it?

A lot of companies push replacements because the ticket is bigger. We'll tell you which one actually makes sense for your situation — and here's how to think about it yourself.

REPAIR MAKES SENSE WHEN

Fix what you have

The system is under 10–12 years old
It's a single known issue (capacitor, fan motor, refrigerant)
Repair cost is under 40% of a new system
The system has been well-maintained
Eddie has fixed ACs in under 20 minutes that other companies quoted a full replacement for. If it can be repaired honestly, that's what we'll recommend.
REPLACE MAKES SENSE WHEN

Start fresh

The system is 15+ years old with recurring issues
Repair costs are stacking up year over year
It uses R-22 refrigerant (phased out, expensive)
Energy bills have been climbing despite maintenance
When replacement is the right call, we right-size the new system to your actual home — not the biggest unit on the truck. Straight pricing, no surprises.

Keep your system healthy

Four things that prevent the emergency call.

A little seasonal attention keeps your AC and heater running right — and keeps Eddie and Tony from seeing you in an emergency.

SPRING / SUMMER

Change the air filter every 30–60 days

A clogged filter is the single most common reason ACs blow warm. Set a phone reminder — it takes 30 seconds and saves hundreds in repair calls.

SPRING / SUMMER

Clear the outdoor condenser

Trim plants back 2 feet from the unit and rinse debris off the fins with a garden hose. A choked condenser works twice as hard and wears out faster.

FALL / WINTER

Test your heater before you need it

Run it for 15 minutes on the first cool day. Strange smells or no heat? Call now while there's no wait — not in December when everyone else does.

YEAR-ROUND

Schedule a yearly tune-up

A professional check-up catches worn parts before they fail. It's the difference between a $150 tune-up and a $1,500 emergency repair in July.

Common questions

What homeowners ask before calling.

How quickly can you get here?
For urgent calls (no cooling in summer heat, for example), Eddie or Tony often arrive the same day — sometimes within a couple of hours. For non-urgent work, we'll schedule you within a few days at a time that works for you.
Do you work on weekends?
We're available Monday through Saturday. Sunday is our one day off. We hold Saturday slots specifically for urgent calls, so if your AC dies on a Friday night, you're not waiting until Monday.
How much does a repair cost?
It depends entirely on what's wrong — a capacitor replacement is very different from a compressor swap. What we can tell you: we diagnose honestly, quote fairly, and we've been told by multiple customers that our prices are "more than reasonable." We never push a replacement when a repair will do.
Should I repair or replace my AC?
Generally: if it's under 12 years old and the repair is a single known issue, repair. If it's 15+ years old with recurring problems or uses the old R-22 refrigerant, replacement usually makes more sense long-term. We break this down in detail in our repair vs. replace section above — and Eddie will always tell you honestly which makes more sense for your situation.
What brands do you service?
All major residential brands — Carrier, Lennox, Trane, Rheem, Goodman, York, Daikin, Bryant, Amana, and more. If it heats or cools your home, Eddie's seen it.
Do I need a tune-up if my system seems fine?
That's actually the best time to get one. A tune-up catches worn parts before they fail — it's the difference between a planned $150 visit and an emergency $1,500 repair in the middle of July. We recommend once a year, ideally before summer.
Are you licensed and insured?
Yes, fully licensed and insured. We're happy to provide documentation if your property manager or HOA requires it.
What areas do you serve?
We cover Ontario and the surrounding Inland Empire — Chino, Chino Hills, Montclair, Upland, Rancho Cucamonga, Pomona, Fontana, and nearby communities. If you're not sure whether you're in our area, call us and we'll let you know.
